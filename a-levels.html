A-Level Subjects

I enjoy learning about all of my chosen subjects. Below, I've gone into detail about why I chose to study each subject.

  • Maths
  • Further Maths
  • Computer Science
  • Physics

Maths

Why did I choose to study maths?

I like problem solving, so therefore I thought that A-Level Maths would be an interesting course.

As well as this, A-Level Maths is a requirement to obtain offers for most Computer Science courses at University. Maths is also a requirement for lots of other University courses, so not only did I take Maths so I could study Computer Science, but it also means that my options for studying other subjects are still open.

Maths events that I've taken part in:

  • Maths and Computer Science open day at Oxford University
  • Senior Maths Challenge
  • RI Maths Masterclasses
  • Maths mentoring/leadership programmes within school

Future maths events that I'm going to be participating in:

  • An event at the University of Southampton aimed at women studying Maths

Further Maths

Why did I choose to study Further Maths?

I taught myself Further Maths at GCSE level and really enjoyed the course since it explored topics I already knew about but in more detail, as well as covering completely new areas within Maths. Teaching myself the Further Maths course at GCSE-level also gave me more confidence in deciding to take Further Maths as a fourth A-Level.

Similar to why I chose Maths, Futher Maths is also a recommended subject to take for Computer Science University courses, and at some universities it is a requirement.

My three favourite topics within the Further Maths course:

  1. Complex numbers
  2. Further calculus
  3. Matrices / matrix transformations

Computer Science

Why did I choose to study Computer Science?

Computer Science really interests me, so I would like to study it at University and then hopefully persue a career in the tech industry.

Careers involving Computer Science / computing fields that interest me:

  • Software developer
  • Artificial intelligence / machine learning
  • Cyber security

Physics

Why did I choose to study Physics?

I really enjoyed Physics at GCSE level, and I think that the subject is very interesting.

Also, I knew I wanted to take a science at A-Level and Physics was my favourite out of the three sciences, so that's another reason why I choose Physics.
